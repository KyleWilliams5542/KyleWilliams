About Us

GOODIFY INC is a trusted supplier serving the education, gardening, and small-scale agriculture sectors across North America. We curate high-quality, accessible products tailored to the unique needs of kindergarten educators, home gardeners, and independent family farmers, with a commitment to sustainability and community impact. For over 12 years, we have prioritized reliable service and solutions that help our customers thrive in their respective pursuits.

Founded in 2012 in Portland, Oregon, GOODIFY INC began with a simple mission: to make high-quality, purpose-built supplies accessible to communities that nurture growth in all its forms. What started as a small local retailer serving regional preschools and home gardeners has expanded into a national supplier, serving over 2,000 customers across the United States and Canada. We work directly with ethical manufacturers and small-batch producers to ensure every product we offer meets strict standards for safety, durability, and environmental responsibility. Our team includes former early childhood educators, master gardeners, and sustainable agriculture specialists who bring real-world expertise to every product recommendation and customer interaction. We are proud to donate 5% of our annual profits to community programs that support school garden initiatives and beginning farmer training, aligning our business success with the well-being of the communities we serve.

Our Work

Kindergarten Classroom Supply Upgrade & Outdoor Learning Garden Installation

Client: Maplewood Early Learning Center, Seattle WA

Maplewood Early Learning Center, a non-profit preschool serving 120 low-income students, sought to update its aging classroom supplies and build a new outdoor learning garden to integrate hands-on environmental education into its curriculum. GOODIFY INC worked with the center’s education team to select developmentally appropriate, safety-tested learning materials and a child-friendly garden kit aligned with their program goals, while providing a 30% non-profit discount to fit their limited budget.

  • Student engagement in STEM and art activities increased by 45% in the first semester following the supply upgrade, per teacher assessments
  • The outdoor learning garden now provides 70% of the fresh produce served in the center’s student lunch program, reducing annual food costs by $3,200

Small-Scale Urban Farm Irrigation & Soil Health Improvement

Client: Sunny Side Urban Farm, Brooklyn NY

Sunny Side Urban Farm, a 2-acre community farm providing affordable local produce to 500 low-income households in Brooklyn, struggled with inconsistent crop yields and high water bills due to inefficient irrigation and depleted urban soil. GOODIFY INC provided a customized portable drip irrigation system and organic soil amendment bundle, alongside free consultation from our agronomist to develop a site-specific crop rotation and soil building plan.

  • Farm water usage decreased by 58% in the first growing season, cutting annual water expenses by $2,100
  • Total vegetable yield increased by 72% year-over-year, allowing the farm to expand its affordable produce program to serve an additional 200 local households

Community Garden Supply Program for Beginner Gardeners

Client: Portland Home Gardener Collective, Portland OR

The Portland Home Gardener Collective, a non-profit organization supporting 300 new home gardeners from immigrant and refugee communities, needed affordable, culturally appropriate gardening supplies and educational resources to help participants grow their own food. GOODIFY INC curated custom seed collections including varieties common to the participants’ home countries, paired with raised bed kits and multilingual planting guides, and donated 20% of the order value in additional supplies to support the program.

  • 92% of program participants reported harvesting enough produce to meet 30% or more of their household vegetable needs within the first growing season
  • The collective expanded its program to serve an additional 150 gardeners the following year, using the successful supply model developed with GOODIFY INC
