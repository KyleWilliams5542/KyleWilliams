Welcome to DXGLIDE INC

DXGLIDE INC is a leading convenience retail operator dedicated to making daily life simpler for communities across North America. With strategically located stores and extended operating hours, we deliver fast, hassle-free access to the items customers need most, whenever they need them.

Founded in 2017, DXGLIDE INC was built on the belief that convenience retail should be reliable, accessible, and customer-centric. We started with a single neighborhood store in Portland, Oregon, and have grown to operate 47 locations across 7 U.S. states, each designed to meet the unique needs of the communities they serve. Our team prioritizes clean, well-organized stores, friendly customer service, and a carefully curated inventory that eliminates unnecessary choices and focuses on the high-quality, affordable items customers use every day. We partner with local suppliers whenever possible to support regional businesses and offer exclusive, community-focused products that resonate with our customers. At DXGLIDE, we don’t just sell products – we build long-term relationships with the people who visit our stores, working every day to be a trusted, essential part of daily life for every neighborhood we join.

Success Stories

24/7 Neighborhood Convenience Store Launch

Client: Metro Portland Residential Community Association

The 1,200-home Cedar Hills residential community in Beaverton, Oregon, had no local retail options within a 1-mile radius, forcing residents to drive 15+ minutes for basic essentials, especially outside of standard grocery store hours. DXGLIDE INC partnered with the community association to open a 1,800 sq. ft. convenience store at the main neighborhood entrance, with operating hours from 6AM to 12AM, 7 days a week. We worked with local leadership to curate inventory specifically for the community’s needs, including more family-sized household items, fresh school lunch options, and local Portland-based snack and beverage brands.

  • 92% of community residents reported reduced travel time for essential purchases, saving an average of 3 hours per household per month
  • Store exceeded first-year revenue projections by 27%, with 83% of customers returning at least once per week

Highway Rest Area Convenience Retail Management

Client: Interstate Highway Rest Area Operator, MidWest Infrastructure Group

MidWest Infrastructure Group operates 12 highway rest areas across Illinois and Indiana, and had received consistent customer feedback about limited, overpriced retail options for travelers. DXGLIDE was selected as the exclusive retail partner for all 12 locations, tasked with redesigning the store layout, curating traveler-focused inventory, and extending operating hours to match rest area 24/7 access. We prioritized road trip-specific items including travel-sized toiletries, energy drinks, road trip snacks, and pre-prepared meals for long-haul drivers and family travelers.

  • Customer satisfaction scores for rest area retail increased from 3.2/5 to 4.7/5 in the first 6 months of operation
  • Overall retail revenue across the 12 locations increased by 62% year-over-year, with 45% of purchases coming from repeat commercial driver customers

On-Campus Convenience Store for Student Housing Complex

Client: University of Arizona Student Housing Department

The University of Arizona’s 3,500-student Canyon Ranch housing complex lacked a dedicated retail option for students, many of whom did not have access to a car and needed late-night access to snacks, school supplies, and essentials during exam periods. DXGLIDE partnered with the university to open a 1,200 sq. ft. store inside the housing complex, with extended hours until 2AM on weekdays and 3AM on weekends. We curated inventory specifically for student needs, including affordable instant meals, school supplies, energy drinks, and discounted bulk snack packs for study groups.

  • 89% of student residents reported using the store at least twice per month, with 61% visiting during late-night hours after other campus retail options had closed
  • Student feedback surveys showed a 38% reduction in reports of food insecurity among housing residents, attributed to access to affordable, late-night meal and snack options
